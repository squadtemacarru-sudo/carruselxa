# 5 Stages of Awareness (Eugene Schwartz)

**Core Principle**: Your copy must match the prospect's current awareness level. Wrong level = ignored copy. Move them one stage at a time.

## Stage 1: UNAWARE
They don't know they have a problem.
- Lead with curiosity or a surprising stat. NO product mention.
- Headline: "La razón oculta por la que [audiencia] no avanza"
- CTA: "Descubrí si esto te pasa a vos"

## Stage 2: PROBLEM AWARE
They feel the pain but don't know solutions exist.
- Name the problem using their exact language. Agitate consequences.
- Headline: "¿Por qué [problema] no desaparece por más que lo intentés?"
- CTA: "Leé cómo otros lo resolvieron"

## Stage 3: SOLUTION AWARE
They know solutions exist but not your specific approach.
- Lead with the mechanism, not the product. Compare approaches.
- Headline: "Por qué [tipo de solución] funciona donde las rutinas genéricas fallan"
- CTA: "Mirá cómo funciona"

## Stage 4: PRODUCT AWARE
They know you exist but haven't bought.
- Specificity is everything: features, diferenciadores, prueba social, objeciones.
- Headline: "Por qué 100+ personas eligieron [marca] sobre el resto"
- CTA: "Escribinos por DM"

## Stage 5: MOST AWARE
They're ready to buy. Just need the push.
- Direct, simple. Remove friction. Add urgency.
- Headline: "[Oferta directa] — Quedan X lugares"
- CTA: "Escribí YA"

## Copy Length Rule
- Unaware/Problem Aware → copy LARGO (educa primero)
- Solution/Product Aware → largo-medio (diferencia y convence)
- Most Aware → CORTO y directo

## For Instagram Carousels
- Slide 1 (hook) = decide el awareness level del carrusel entero
- Si el tema es educativo → Stage 2-3
- Si el tema es oferta/prueba → Stage 4-5
- Nunca mezcles estrategias de dos stages muy distintos en el mismo carrusel
